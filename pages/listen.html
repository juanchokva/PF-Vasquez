Fluchtpuntk (radiogegenteil) 2021-2022

For voice, flute and live electronics. Work comissioned by CG Ensemble (Bogotá), dedicated to Beatriz Elena Martínez, Laura Cubides and Rodolfo Acosta R. World premiere at the «En el acto de sonar» Festival (Bogotá) 05/03/2022. For an optimal listening, use headphones (Binaural)

cap i cua (cielo y raíces) 2019

Electroacoustic piece. Dedicated to the memory of Graciela Vargas. Its materials, without planning, were recorded in La Vega, Colombia, her birthplace. The work was conceived as a return to what is essential, as a path that, even if appears to go straight, returns to common places: It travels to locations already visited and resignifies them. Have a good journey, dear Grandma.

Calíope (canto de las abejas) 2014

For guitar and electronics. Piece composed and dedicated to the slovenian guitarist Primož Sukič. Live recording of the concert in Ljubljana (05.04.14). The piece could be taken as a sound sculpture created by a gesture that evolves in time, and some modified replications of it produced via live electronics. The guitar is taken then as one dimension of a constructed reality, while the replicas could be taken as variations or disturbances of this reality.

Verschleierte Spiegel 2014

For violin solo. Recording made by Juan Carlos Higuita, colombian violinist, as a part of his CD 8 Mundos. Verschleierte Spiegel is the result of a reflection about Jorge Luis Borges’ ideas about mirrors and how they deform reality instead of reflecting it. This piece was composed for Ensemble Recherche’s project «The future begins at 19:15» and it is dedicated to the great violinist Melise Mellinger.

Keine Gitarre! 2011

For guitar six hands. Recording made by Amuki Ensamble (Bolivia).Piece composed for CLIC, experimental music Ensemble from Bogotá (Colombia).

En el alba 2010

For voice and electronics. Work composed and dedicated to Beatriz Elena Martínez, colombian singer.«At dawn -said the poet- I remembered myself saying some words that at first I didn’t recognize. Those words are a poem.» JORGE LUIS BORGES. El libro de arena.
